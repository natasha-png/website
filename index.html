My Why:

Born and raised in Hyderabad, India, Natasha Valluri is an intersectional feminist and paneer enthusiast studying in Computational Media at Georgia Tech. She believes in declaring missions, not majors and works in the intersection of computer science, design and social good to make education accessible, inclusive and personalized all over the world.

At Georgia Tech, she has been involved with Pride Alliance- the undergraduate LGBTQIA+ organization- to create designs and curate events that foster community. She is currently at Bits of Good, where she oversees operations as the Associate Executive Director for the student-run organization that partners with non-profit organizations across Atlanta to build technology-oriented solutions to their problems.

Outside of Georgia Tech, she loves spending time with her organization- WiTech (Youth for Women in Technology Inc.)- where she serves as the Executive Director. WiTech has established its presence through more than 25 chapters all over the world, with more than 400 high school and college students working to provide access to technology to everyone- despite their gender or socioeconomic status. Constantly curious and conditioned to care, Natasha is driven to build communities and creations around accessible and universal technology and design.
